“When the whole world is moving towards automation…it might be a good idea to learn to code!”


Allow me to introduce myself. My name is Mo Zahid. I’m a Software Engineering Student from New York City. I’m also an environmental enthusiast, a yogi, weightlifter, martial artist (Brazilian Jiu Jitsu) and meditator.


Thank you for taking the time to visit my website. Let me tell you a little bit about the recent events in my life that have led me down the coding path. After attending Rutgers University for finance I went on to first become an Accountant and then a Financial Analyst. After spending a few years in the financial sector I had identified some major trends. The whole bank seemed to be investing heavily in automation. Whole departments were beginning to shrink and reduce headcount. Competition was increasing and compensation was decreasing. I myself had been put on countless projects to automate certain functions. It was not long before I realized I could not ignore the signs. My role in this sector was not in a state of expansion but rather there was a great contraction taking place. One of my mentors always told me that my skills should be relevant for the times. So in an effort to remain adaptive I began to come up with a plan for a career shift. It seemed like a really good time to re-engage with my network to gather information on the possibilities within the tech world. I did not have a whole lot of information, but I did know the direction I wanted to go in. If automation was increasing then technology would be a good place to start.


After setting up a total of 57 phone calls I had started to get a clearer idea for the opportunities out there. A few software engineers in my network described a rewarding profession. At an early age I had always had an interest in computer science and coding so their words really resonated with me. Long story short I quit my job to pursue this new path full time and the rest is history. From April 26th to August 5th 2021 I will be engaged in a software engineering Boot Camp at the Flatiron School!
